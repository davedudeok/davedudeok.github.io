---
title: "the silent librarian: cron and obsidian"
date: 2026-03-30
---

i used to waste so much time manually pushing commits every time i finished a thought in obsidian. it was friction—a small, annoying tax on creativity that made me want to close the editor rather than write one more line.

then i remembered cron. 

it’s the classic unix tool, usually reserved for system admin maintenance and server health checks. but here, it’s my personal librarian. i just wrote a tiny shell script that looks at my vault, checks for changes, and pushes them to github. i set it to run in the background every 15 minutes.

that's it. no more staging files, no more typing git messages when i just want to capture an idea. 

the site stays updated, the git history remains clean, and i get to stay in the zone. the lesson: automation isn't just for deployment pipelines or server clusters. when you remove the friction between your thoughts and your public presence, you stop treating writing like a task and start treating it like a stream. 

my silent librarian doesn't sleep, and neither does my site.
